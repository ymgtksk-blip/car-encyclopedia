Citroën ZX 2.0i 16V

The Citroën ZX (launched 1991, France) was a PSA Peugeot-Citroën compact hatchback built on the platform later shared with the Peugeot 306. Its sportiest mainstream version was the 2.0i 16V, powered by the 1,998 cc XU10J4 twin-cam 16-valve four-cylinder producing 150 PS (110 kW) DIN and driving the front wheels through a 5-speed manual. At around 1,150 kg kerb weight it was a genuine hot hatch, sprinting to 100 km/h in about 9.4 seconds and reaching a top speed near 220 km/h. The ZX was praised for its excellent ride and handling thanks to its passive rear-steer trailing-arm suspension.

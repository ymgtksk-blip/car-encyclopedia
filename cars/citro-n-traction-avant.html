Traction Avant 11CV (11 Légère / 11 BL)

The Citroën Traction Avant, launched in 1934, was a landmark car: one of the first mass-produced vehicles combining front-wheel drive, a unitary (monocoque) steel body with no separate chassis, and independent torsion-bar suspension. This entry covers the 11CV "11 Légère" (11 BL), the compact-bodied version of the 11CV powered by Citroën's 1,911 cc (78 x 100 mm) inline-four with overhead valves, driving the front wheels through a 3-speed manual gearbox. Pre-war 11CV cars were rated around 46 hp and reached roughly 110-112 km/h. The Traction Avant stayed in production, largely unchanged in concept, until 1957 and is regarded as one of the most influential cars of the 20th century.

Citroën Dyane 6

The Citroën Dyane launched in 1967 as a more modern, better-equipped 2CV derivative, sharing its platform, air-cooled flat-twin engine and soft interconnected suspension but with a full rear hatchback. The definitive Dyane 6, from 1968, used the Ami's 602 cc M4 flat-twin producing about 28 PS and ~42 N·m, with a top speed near 110–115 km/h. A front-engine, front-wheel-drive economy car weighing only ~585–600 kg, it stayed in production until 1983. No reputable 0–100 km/h or Japanese MSRP figure exists.

Fiat 127

The Fiat 127 is an Italian supermini launched in April 1971, replacing the 850 and winning the 1972 European Car of the Year. This Series 1 has a transverse 903 cc OHV inline-four driving the front wheels via a 4-speed manual, making 47 PS DIN at 6200 rpm and 62 N·m at 3800 rpm, with a kerb weight of about 705 kg. Praised for space efficiency, the 1971 launch body was a 2-door saloon; a 3-door hatchback followed in March 1972. Built at Fiat's Mirafiori plant in Turin.

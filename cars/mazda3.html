Mazda Axela Sport 23S (Mazda3 BK)

The first-generation Mazda3 (Japan: Mazda Axela) debuted at Frankfurt in 2003 and launched in Japan in October 2003 as a global C-segment sedan and 5-door hatchback ("Axela Sport") on Mazda's Ford-shared C1/BK platform. The chosen variant is the JDM Axela Sport 23S (chassis UA-BK3P, 5MT), the naturally-aspirated sporty top grade. It uses the 2.3L MZR L3-VE inline-four (2261 cc; JDM catalogs round to 2260) rated 171 PS (126 kW) at 6500 rpm and 214 N.m (21.8 kgf.m) at 4000 rpm, all to the front wheels. Kerb weight is 1270 kg for the 5MT (4AT 1280 kg). Ratings are JIS net, not SAE-gross; the same NA L3-VE was rated 164 hp/122 kW (SAE-net) in export markets. The 23S sat below the later 2.3 DISI turbo Mazdaspeed3; lesser grades used the 1.5L (118 PS) and 2.0L MZR (150 PS). The BK ran 2003-2009 before the BL replaced it.

Mercedes-Benz ML 320 (W163)

The Mercedes-Benz M-Class (W163) was the marque's first modern SUV, unveiled on 21 May 1997 and built at the new Tuscaloosa, Alabama plant. The launch ML 320 uses a 3.2-litre M112 SOHC V6 with 218 PS (160 kW) at 5,600 rpm and 310 N·m at 3,000 rpm, a 5-speed automatic and permanent 4MATIC with low range. Kerb weight about 1,990 kg; 0-100 km/h roughly 9.5 s. It paired car-like comfort with genuine off-road ability.

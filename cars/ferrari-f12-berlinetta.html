Ferrari F12berlinetta

The Ferrari F12berlinetta is a front-mid-engine, rear-wheel-drive V12 grand tourer / supercar that replaced the 599 GTB Fiorano. Its naturally aspirated 6.3-litre (6,262 cc) 65-degree F140-family V12 produces 740 PS (544 kW) at 8,250 rpm and 690 N·m of torque at 6,000 rpm, making it the most powerful naturally aspirated road-going Ferrari at its 2012 Geneva Motor Show launch. Ferrari quotes 0-100 km/h in 3.1 seconds and a top speed over 340 km/h. Kerb weight is 1,630 kg (dry weight 1,525 kg), with a rear-biased 54% weight distribution. It was succeeded by the 812 Superfast in 2017. Figures are the DIN/EEC-net European ratings for the base variant, not the later F12tdf.

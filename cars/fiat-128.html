Fiat 128

The Fiat 128, launched in 1969, was Fiat's first mass-produced front-engine, transverse, front-wheel-drive car. Its layout became the template for nearly every FWD car and won the 1970 European Car of the Year. The Berlina used an all-new 1116 cc SOHC four designed under Aurelio Lampredi, making 55 PS (DIN) at 6000 rpm and about 77 N·m, driving a 4-speed manual. Praised for roominess and sharp handling, it was built and licensed worldwide (Zastava, SEAT, Nasr).

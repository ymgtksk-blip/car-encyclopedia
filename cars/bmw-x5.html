BMW X5 4.4i (E53)

The BMW X5 (E53) was BMW's first SUV, a "Sports Activity Vehicle," debuting at the 1999 North American International Auto Show with production starting in 1999. The chosen variant is the launch flagship X5 4.4i, powered by the M62TUB44 4.4-litre naturally aspirated DOHC V8 (4398 cc) making 286 PS (210 kW) at 5400 rpm and 440 Nm at 3600 rpm, with a 5-speed Steptronic automatic and permanent all-wheel drive. BMW quotes 0-100 km/h in 7.5 s and an electronically limited top speed of about 207-210 km/h. BMW is a German marque (country DE) and the E53 was engineered in Germany, though built at BMW's Spartanburg plant in South Carolina, USA. This 286 PS M62 V8 ran from launch through 2003; the 2004 facelift replaced it with the N62 4.4i rated at 320 PS. The 4.4i was the on-road-biased luxury halo of the early range, above the 3.0i and below the later 4.6is.

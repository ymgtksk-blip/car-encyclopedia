Sierra 2.0 GL (Mk I)

The Ford Sierra Mk I, launched in 1982 as the aerodynamic successor to the Cortina/Taunus, was a rear-wheel-drive family car by Ford of Europe. This 2.0 GL uses the 1993 cc Pinto SOHC inline-four carburettor engine making 105 PS at ~5200 rpm and 157 N·m at 4000 rpm (DIN net). Its jelly-mould styling was radical and controversial, but low drag made it a strong European seller. Top speed about 185 km/h; 5-door kerb weight roughly 1,100 kg. Ran until the 1987 facelift.
